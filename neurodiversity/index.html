Introduction

When talking about or discussing neurodiversity as a society, there tend to be a lot of ideas surrounding a few of these neurotypes that are not true. Now, I’m no doctor or expert, but as a member of the neurodiverse community myself, I created this website in hopes that it might give you a bit more clarification on what a few of the neurotypes are. I’ve picked out the following three neurotypes as they are the most commonly discussed and encountered ones (in my experience); ADHD (Attention Defecit Hyperactivity Disorder), ASD (Autsim Spectrum Disorder), and Dyslexia, as well as OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder), as it has connections to neurodiverse neurotypes, and is also a condition that I frequently see ignorance about.

Please keep in mind that the following pages might contain personal or sensitive information and topics.

What is Neurodiversity?

Neurodiversity is the range or variety of differences in individual brains and the way they typically process information. Neurodiversity is the diverse abilities of the brain, or how different the brain’s abilities are, when compared to a neurotypical (the typical) brain.

Seeking Help and Guidance

Remember, neurotypes aren’t diseases, but living with them can sometimes (or all the time) be a great struggle. If you feel like you are neurodiverse, are already diagnosed as neurodiverse, or want to know more about your neurotype, don’t be afraid to reach out. Below are some links to places/people that will give you the help you need or can give you more information and support for your neurotype.
